Flags/penalties



5 yard penalties


Defensive holding: Offensive player being held by a defensive player by the uniform instead of blocking, which prevents the offensive player from moving in a certain direction.

Defensive offside: When the ball is snapped, and a defensive player is across the line of scrimage.

Delay of game: Usually when the play clock is out and the ball isn't snapped.

Delay of kickoff: Same thing as a delay of game, expect it's during the kickoff.

Encroachment: A defensive player crosses the line of scrimmage illegall, and eother find a clear path to the Quarterback or touches an offensive player

Excess timeouts: When any player has excessive celebrations. 5 or 15 yards.

False start: Offensive player starts before the ball is snapped.

Illegal formation: At the time of the snap, players are in illegal formation.

Illegal forward handoff: After a forward pass, there is an illegal forward handoff.

Illegal forward pass: Same thing as illegal foward handoff except with a forward pass. Or when the passer makes a pass beyond the line of scrimmage.

Illegal kick and kicking loose ball: Player kicking a loose ball or kicks it when they are inable to make contact.

Illegal motion: Player shifts/moves in an illegal manner before the ball is snapped.

Illegal shift: Same thing as illegal formation, but it's a shift instead of formation

Illegal snap: Snap done incorrectly. Example ball isn't on the ground when it's snapped.

Illegal substitution: Player is substituted illegaly. Either the play is live or interferes with the play.

Illegal touching of a Forward pass: If there is a forward pass only eligible receivers can catch the ball.

Inelegible downfield kick: When anyone crosses the line of scrimmage prior to the kick.

Inelegible pass downfield: An ineleligible reciever completes a pass downfield.

Invalid fair catch signal: A returner calls a fair catch the wrong way.

Neutral zone infraction: A defensive player illegally crosses the line of scrimmage into the neutral zone, before the snap.

Offensive offside: Same thing as neutral zone infraction except on offense.

Offside on free kick: Offside on a free kick.

Pass interference: A receiver or someone catching the ball gets hindered by another player.

Player out of bounds on kick: When someone who is out of bounds re-enters the playing field.

Running into the kicker: Making contact with the kickers pant of leg on a punt or kick.

Sideline infraction: Coach or player exits restricted area.

Too many players on field: When a team has more than 11 players on field.


10 yard penalties


Assisting the runner: Helping the runner in a certain direction by a push or a pull.

Illegal bat: Batting the ball when its lose on purpose.

Illegal block above the waist: Blocking a player who is not the ball carrier above and behind his waist.

Illegal block in the back: Blocking opponents from behind, above the waist.

Illegal use of hands: Basically hands to the face when trying to block.

Intentional grounding: When the passer throws the ball away with intention to avoid a sack.

Offensive holding: Vise versa of defensive holding.

Offensive pass interference: When an offensive player prevents a defensive player from making a play on the ball.

Tripping: When one player trips another player intentionally.


Major Penalties

15 yards or more


Blocking a kicking team player out of bounds: A kick is blocked from out of bounds.

Chop block: One offensive player blocks below the thigh and another one blocks below the thigh of a defenisve player.

Clipping: Falling onto an opposing player from below the knees.

Pass Interference: A receiver or someone catching the ball gets hindered by another player.

Defenseless player: A player makes unnecessary contact on a player in a defenseless position.

Disconcerting acts or signals: Disconcering the offensive team pior to snap.

Disqualification: Getting removed due to their actions.

Excessive celebration: Calling two timeouts on the same dead ball.

Facemask: When one player grabs the facemask of an opposing player.

Fair catch interference: When a kicking team player interferes wth a returning player during a fair catch.

Forcibly contacting a player out of bounds: When a player pushes the runner when already out of bounds.

Goaltending: Jumping to deflect a kicked ball so it doesn't go over the crossbar.

Horse collar: Players grabs an opposing player right of their feet.

Illegal blindside block: Blocking an opposing player while the player is incapable of seeing the blocker.

Illegal block after fair catch signal: A player of the kicking team is blocked by a player from the receiving team before the ball is caught.

Illegal block below the waist: DUring a change of possesion, one player blocks another player below the waist.

Illegal crackback: Player runs laterally and blocks a defenseless player below their waist.

Illegal cut block: Diving into the knees of an opposing player from the front.

Illegal double team block: Multiple players block the same guy at the same time, but they were not lined up together on the setup line.

Illegal peel back: Blocking a player below the waist from the side.

Illegal wedge: Multiple players lining up shoulder to shoulder to perform a block less than 2 yards apart.

Kick catch intereference: A player on the kicking team runs into a receiver before the ball is actually live.

Leaping: Jumping over the line of scrimmage to block the ball and makes contact with another player.

Leverage: Making contact with an opposing player with getting a boost over the line of scrimmage.

Low block: Illegal block below the waist of an opposing player.

Removal of helmet: Player removes helmet during a celebration.

Roughing the holder: Defensive player comes into contact with the holder.

Roughing the kicker: Player makes contact with the kickers lower body.

Roughing the Passer: Defensive player makes contact with the passer after the ball has been thrown.

Taunting: One player taunts an opponent.

Unnecessary physical contact with a game official: Contact is made with an official.

Unnecessary roughness: When contact is made to an opponent and the contact is unnecessary.

Unsportsmanlike conduct: Violating the sportsmanship rule.

Use of helmet: Using a helmet to make contact with an opposing player.
